I've been referring to spherical heating elements throughout the article so far, but actually that may not be the best shape for such an application. After all, one would want to physically press up a heating element snugg against a receiving element in order to transfer the heat through conduction effectively. But spheres are inherently difficult to press up against anything, which is why they tend to roll so well. So really we want flat surfaces on our heating elements for this reason. A cube may thus work as a better suited shape for the heating element.



Well actually we can break the cube up into many flat layers, which are held together as a cube during transport and heating, but which can also be seperated from each other, and is each one a heating element in it's own right.
